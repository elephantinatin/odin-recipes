Takeaway from JustEat

Description

It doesn't have to be XFC, takeaway can be all sorts of disgusting, guilt-inducing, slippery food. It can also be good food, but that's not what we look for in takeaway, is it?

This recipe takes very little time and effort but might lead to gentle, arhythmic heart palpitatons, shallow and rough breathing, and the lingering smell of fried despair.

Ingredients

  • Lethargy
  • Empty cupboards
  • Some throwaway cash
  • Encouragement to "Go on, treat yourself!"
  • Unused sauce packets from previous takeaways

Steps

  1. Give up.
  2. Log into JustEat, trying not to pay too much attention to the feasts you've recently ordered from that Chinese place run by Sri Lankans.
  3. Spend the next hour filling four baskets from four different "restaurants".
  4. Work out which has the highest quantity of food for the lowest price and order.
  5. Every five minutes, check to see how far your order has been processed.
  6. Wait with an anxiety and readiness that will have you panicking to answer the door.
  7. Exchange awkward pleasantries with the delivery guy.
  8. Present whatever food you bought on a fancy plate to make it feel like home cooking.
  9. Start experience feelings of regret just before you're half your way through eating.
  10. Keep eating.
  11. Lean back and try not to move too much for an indefinite period of time.
