Dorm Room Ramen Noodles

Description

This recipe was made by my mother, a true artisan in the kitchen. She used to serve it to us in weird plates that turned up at the sides, creating a concave surface.

Suitable for any dormitory-dwelling dude (or dudette) who is determined to do diddly-squat in order to dine.

Ingredients

  • 1 packet of 23p ramen noodles
  • 1 little powder packet that comes with it
  • 1 oil pouch thing that sometimes pops out unexpectedly
  • 1 cup tap water
  • [optional] your choice of vegetables

Steps

  1. Buy 23p ramen noodles from your local newsagents.
  2. Go home and open the packet. Pour the contents into a deep plate.
  3. Before the previous step, put tap water in the kettle and make it boil.
  4. Eat a few crumbles of the dry ramen.
  5. Empty some of the hot water into the half-spherical plate containing dry ramen.
  6. Wait.
  7. Stop waiting. Empty the sachets of powder and, if you're lucky, the oil into the wet ramen.
  8. Use any protruding utensil or digit to stir the ramen.
  9. Add more stuff if you want.
  10. Serve it in the large cup plate you had already put it in.
